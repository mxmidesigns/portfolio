GOCARDS

The best way to learn on the go, customized just for you.

THE PROBLEM

It can be difficult to keep track of all the jargon when studying a new discipline. Students need a way to methodically categorize, reference, and study new vocabulary and concepts. How might we design a mobile app that empowers people to learn new vocabulary?


The SOLUTION

By creating an app that uses visuals, audio, and videos with reviewing activities that shows progress, rewards, and reminders, it will help students move forward confidently in their field.


TOOLS

Invision (Prototype), Sketch (Wireframes and Visual Design)


View Prototype

DISCOVERY

COMPETITIVE ANALYSIS

Who else is out there?

I used competitive analysis to better understand the problem and to get me thinking about some solutions that have already been proposed to address the problem. I looked at 3 mobile apps that focus on learning new vocabulary: Magoosh, Quizlet Flashcard, and Memrise.

KEY FINDINGS

  1. All apps focused a lot on the visuals and audio to enhance the learning.
  2. By incorporating learning games and rewards, it enables users to learn new words in an addicting and fun way by giving them a sense of accomplishment.
  3. The ability to pick your level lets the app not be too challenged or too easy which gives the app more sense of interest to the user and help them learn.
  4. Some apps allow users to build their vocabulary by reading and listening games.

USER INTERVIEW

Getting to know my users.

I conducted user interviews to gain insights into the needs and wants of users during their learning process. My research goal was to better understand how people approach learning a new subject and vocabulary. The interviews included students and working professionals.

OBSERVATIONS

  1. Success for people is due to perseverance and repetition.
  2. Difficult to learn something new if users have no interest in it.
  3. Studying is an on-going process and it takes time to figure things out.

KEY INSIGHTS

  1. Easier to learn with visuals because there are images to reference. Words and visuals have a great way of conveying a meaning across.
  2. Looks at mobile app like Duolingo and YouTube videos to learn.
  3. Users like to engage with visuals and audio and less reading.
  4. Users look for apps that are free and easy to use on the go.
  5. Absorbs more knowledge and less likely to get distracted when visuals are nice to look at.

PERSONAS

Based on my user interviews, I have cumulated all common personality traits and compiled the data and mix them together to create the ideal user who I am designing my app for. My app is aimed at young-savvy professionals.

CONCEPTING

USER JOURNEY

Shaping Ideas

Keeping Whitney in mind, I mapped her user behavioral flow from opening the app to reviewing her study sets. Understanding their decision making and flow within her study experience allowed me to brainstorm the neccessary feature and oportunities required for Whitney to successully navigate through the app.

USER FLOWS

I created a user flow for the key tasks within the app: onboarding and registration, create custom vocabulary study sets and create flashcards. By going through the flow of the key tasks it helped me think through the core activities and ensure a positive process from users.

Usability Testing

Is it useful and functinal?

Before perfecting the app to pixel-perfect, I wanted to make sure that users were able to use the navigation and use the basic functionality of the GoCard App.

GOALS + METHODOLOGY:

  1. The aim of the test is to take a closer look at the main functionalities of the app to determine its overall usefulness.
  2. Identify any problems in order to improve these elements prior further testing.
  3. I hope to understand my user's needs and gain new clarity regarding the navigation of the current app prototype.
  4. The usability tests will be conducted both moderated in-peron. Participants will use my iphone 8+

USER TESTING TASKS:

  1. Create new account.
  2. Add new study sets.
  3. Create new flashcards to study sets.
  4. Review and test new learnings.
Prioritized Issues To Be Addressed
Issue Severity Suggested Change Evidence
Finding study sets quickly. HIGH Add a search bar so users can access their study sets quicker. Users wanted to find their study set without scrolling down too much.
Users find that there were too many clicks to get to the next step. HIGH Make the next destination screen to be the same study set so users can easily add flash cards next. After creating a new study set, users prefer to stay in the same folder to add words faster rather then the previous screen.
Missing dictation feature LOW Add a dictation feature. Users mentioned if they also use their voice to enter text.
Bookmarking HIGH Have a starred feature where users can bookmark the study sets they need to focus more on. Users want the ability to highlight or bookmark study sets that they have the most trouble with.
Editing MEDIUM Have an edit flashcards option so users can quickly edit as they review Users wanted to search quickly and edit words along the way.
