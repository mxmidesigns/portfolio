Fitted

THE PROBLEM

Finding exercise routines for your level can be difficult, especially if you want to try something new and finding routines that fit into your schedule is not easy.


The SOLUTION

I can encourage people who want to exercise get into physical activities by providing easy routines, guides, interactive examples, and info.


TOOLS

Marvelapp (Prototype), Sketch (Wireframes and Visual Design)


View Prototype

DISCOVERY

USER RESEARCH

Getting to know my users' needs.

My research goal was to better understand how people like to work out, what discourages them, and what motivates them to keep going.

OBSERVATIONS

  1. People who are new or returning to fitness want to find activities they like, and get into a good routine.
  2. Users like to search and view routines, guides, daily challenges, and other information on any device. They want to also keep a schedule by adding sessions to their personal calendar.
  3. Users aim to get into a routine that suits them and need an app they can use whenever they like.
  4. When on the go or when practicing exercise routines at home, in the park, on the street, users want to stay fit anywhere

KEY INSIGHTS

  1. Users want to become healthy and enjoy the associated benefits (better mood, weight management, reduce risks of illness, learning something new).
  2. Exercise should be fun and suited to each user
  3. Fitting exercise into daily routines, such as walking or cycling to work or school

PERSONA

USER FLOWS

I created a user flow for the key tasks within the app: search and filter exercise videos, exercise scheduler, add sessions to calendar, create account, tracking progress, daily challenges/rewards, and social sharing.

User Stories

Sketching & Brainstorming

Based on the user flow diagram, I sketched out mobile screens that would meet the user stories' requirements.

Low Fidelity Wireframes

Using Grids

I created low-fidelity 12 column wireframes for my responsive web app that that convey the key functionality the users would need.

Mid Fidelity Wireframes

Composition & Visual Hierarchy = More Intuitive

Considering the key action I wanted users to take, I applied visual hierarchy to my web app screens. I focused on size, contrast, boldness, positioning, and then refining my design by applying good spacing. After arranging the elements into a visual hierarchy that makes sense to my users, I made my design more recognizable and clear to users.

Visual Direction

Setting the mood

Before I begin to add my high fidelity design to my wireframe, I created a mood board to communicate the visual direction of my project.

I wanted to go with a colorful, more playful approach to the fitness web app. By focusing on brighter colors, bold typography, and colorful patterns, the overall app will have an inviting and less intimidating atmosphere to help users have fun while they work out.

Typography

Readable and Easy to Understand

I used SF Pro Text because I wanted to stay consistent with the iOS guidelines for the main typeface. I used Righteous for the logo font because it is clean, readable, and has that retro look for the fitted app style

Color

Evoking emotional responses

For my fitness app, I wanted to use colors that are bright and expressive to evoke emotions of positivity without overwhelming the user. The color orange conveys energy and vitality so it is utilized primarily for the logo and CTAs. The purple evokes feelings of luxury attributed to user achievement.
