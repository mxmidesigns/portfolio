INK TANK

Helping you find your next tattoo.

THE PROBLEM

Getting a tattoo is an incredibly personal experience and the overall process of exploring designs and finding an artist could take months. Getting Inked is a big decision and not many web or mobile apps address the complexity and emotional element of finding the right design and tattoo artist. How can I help people to find what they are looking for in an effort to reduce regret and empower people to express themselves in a meaningful way?


The SOLUTION

The app should contain a platform that has high visual content where users can explore tattoo designs by tattoo artists from locally and globally. Users can direct message their tattoo artist to build a closer connection with their tattoo artist. The designs can be search by art style and the design structure should be intuitive where users can explore easily.

DISCOVERY

COMPETITIVE ANALYSIS

Who else is out there?

I used competitive analysis to learn about the landscape and learn from other apps' strategies. I looked at 2 types of Tattoo apps: Tattoodo and Tattoo Planet.

KEY FINDINGS

  1. Offer features to book consultations with reputable tattoo studios around the world with the click of a button.
  2. With tattoo articles, artist's spotlights, and interviews, users can fully grasps their knowledge on tattoos and find the perfect artist for them.
  3. Competitors have calendar feature where they can make appointment and messaging platform.
  4. Need an advanced search function and scheduling appointment function.

USER INTERVIEW

Getting to know my users.

Before diving into the features and overall product, I conducted user interviews to better understand the problem. My research goal was to Understand the friction points in the tattoo experience and identify solutions to ease them, determine how users search for tattoo designs and tattoo artists, and Identify what features users desire the most. The interviews included people who are considering getting a tattoo, tattoo enthusiasts, and tattoo professionals.

OBSERVATIONS

  1. There’s always consistent communication with the tattoo artist and clients. Tattoo artists always go back and forth with consultation and revision follow-up appointments.
  2. Most users prefer to search for things on Instagram and follow similar artists for awhile before making a decision.
  3. People often get tattoos for social reasons to give them a sense of belonging.

KEY INSIGHTS

  1. Users do a lot of research online through social media to find art content.
  2. People like to follow their tattoo artist on social media and make their decisions after they keep liking their work for some time. People also like to see who their tattoo artists follow as well to find more similar designs.
  3. People try to look for the tattoo artist’s phone number or email to contact them for appointments. A direct messaging can help users to get to know their tattoo artist better before they even visit the shop.
  4. Sometimes people regret their tattoos because they didn't’t like how their artists designed it. There could be lack of communication or people don’t feel comfortable asking questions in person.
  5. Tattoo artist have to consistently stay updated with their appointments and it could get overwhelming. Having a way to track multiple appointments would be valuable.

USER JOURNIES

Shaping Ideas

Understanding the decision making and flow within the tattoo experience allowed me to brainstorm the neccessary feature and oportunities required for Lauren and Danny to successully navigate through the app.

INFORMATION ARCHITECTURE

By analyzing Lauren and Danny's tattoo journey, I was then able to narrow down the features via card sorting for the necessary features for Ink Tank to function. I created a site map to organize the pages, information, and nagivation of the app.

LEARNINGS

  1. Some participants grouped check messages under “Profile” or “Notification”
  2. A participant expected to see “Terms of Use” when signing up first.
  3. Some participants expect to view their upcoming appointments or reschedule appointments under “Profile” or “Notification”
  4. From the Best Merge Result, search, setting, and home were the main categories.

IMPROVEMENTS

  1. The Participant-centric Analysis and Best Merge method were very useful in analyzing my participants’ patterns. With more participants, it could have produced better results.
  2. The top pages have to include search and settings.
  3. Viewing appointment status will be moved to “Profile”

Usability Testing

Is it useful and functinal?

I tested the wireframes against key tasks with the Invision prototype. This helped me identify user frustrations I needed to address so I could improve my designs. For a detailed look at the Test Script, feel free to explore the complete script: here

GOALS + METHODOLOGY:

  1. The aim of the test is to take a closer look at the main functionalities of the app to determine its overall usefulness.
  2. Identify any problems in order to improve these elements prior further testing.
  3. I hope to understand my user's needs and gain new clarity regarding the navigation of the current app prototype.
  4. The usability tests will be conducted both moderated in-peron and moderated remote methods. Participants will use my iphone 8+ and Skype will be used to conduct the test in a moderated remote method.

USER TESTING TASKS:

  1. Imagine you are exploring through the app and you found a tattoo collection you like and would like to bookmark it.
  2. You want to create a new collection for your tattoo ideas.
  3. You are searching for tattoo pictures and found one you want to save.
  4. You found your tattoo artist and would like to schedule an appointment.
Prioritized Issues To Be Addressed
Issue Severity Suggested Change Evidence
‘Tattoos’ and ’Find Artist’ on the bottom navigation are confusing HIGH Consolidate the two search functions. Remove ‘Find Artist’ tab, and combine it with ‘Tattoos’ tab. Users were confused if they had to browse for specific styles of tattoos first and then choose an artist or vice versa. Most users were lost on the navigation and were not sure if the search show the same result views.
‘Saving Picture’ process has too many clicks HIGH Consolidate and simplify menus and interactions. Add a long hold so users can easily and quickly save pictures instead of going to a new screen.  This will reduce the amount of steps it takes to complete an action. Users mentioned the process had too many taps and wanted to quickly save images without having to open the image in a new screen.
Editing date when choosing appointment date MEDIUM Make the calendar date locked and grayed out and move the ’calendar edit’ icon next to the date. This will help users know where to click. Users were tapping on the date instead of the ‘calendar edit’ icon to change dates.
Location of ‘Add New Collection’ HIGH Move ‘Add New Collection’ button closer to the collections. Majority of the users could not find the button to add a new collection or took many random taps to figure out.
More Filters. Search engine does not include pricing LOW Include pricing in the search engine Users wanted a way to filter by price when searching for artists.
